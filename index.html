Open digital tagging of dis/interested spaces

No tag exists with that keyword
  1. Take a sticker and place it somewhere that is meaningful to you. Especially important are places of DIS/INTEREST - those which are overlooked by conventional mapping technologies but are nonetheless important.
  2. Choose a unique TAGWORD and write it on the front of the sticker. Then, scan the QR code to be taken to the web app. There, you can add your new tag.
  3. Anyone who finds your tag will now be able to scan it and leave their own message, for yourself and others to find.

Increasingly, we view our world through privately owned, profit-driven lenses - the services we use to navigate, communicate, and understand our environment. As the portion of our experiences which are filtered through these technologies grows, our physical landscape begins to resemble a digital one. The isolated spaces we occupy online become analogous to the routines in our daily lives informed by our use of proprietary technologies.

We hope that it is possible to disrupt these cycles, by creating a more open sort of digital map - one which gives the power of portraying a place to the people who live in it, and keeps itself firmly grounded through the use of analog tags - stickers.

Over time, these tags will become a collective mapping of experiences - a technological lens on the city which is not tinted by profit motive.

At the outset, each tag will be simply a bulletin board of sorts where people can leave short impressions, advice, or opinions. But we hope to add questions which any visitor can answer, and whose responses we can use to more organically understand our city - ‘do you feel comfortable in this space?’, ‘how gentrified is it here?’, ‘what does this space mean to you, in a word?’.
